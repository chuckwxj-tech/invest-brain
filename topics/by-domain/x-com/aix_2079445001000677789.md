💾 Memory Market Brief | July 21, 2026
（📍Data From https://t.co/rtKhA53pFF Daily Reort）

The memory sector is entering a new phase as pricing expectations, AI demand, and infrastructure investment continue to reshape the outlook.
🧵👇

📉 Memory Prices

• Industry reports suggest memory prices could decline 50–70% during 2026, signaling improving supply conditions after an extended period of tightness.

🔧 Technology Shift

• Samsung Electronics, SK hynix, and Micron have reportedly all stepped back from developing their own CXL controllers, highlighting a strategic shift in next-generation memory architecture.

🤖 AI & Demand

• Some market reports suggest memory shortages may gradually ease, while concerns over OpenAI's financial sustainability have sparked debate about potential risks to future AI infrastructure spending.

📊 Wall Street Views

• Morgan Stanley views the recent selloff in U.S. memory stocks as a healthy reset rather than the start of a new downcycle.

• CLSA remains bullish on SK hynix, reiterating a High Conviction Outperform rating and expecting hyperscale cloud providers to significantly increase capital spending through 2027.

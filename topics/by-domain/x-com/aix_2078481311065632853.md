## 原文

@jukan05 Even if supply shortages persist beyond 2027, the LTAs don’t demonstrate the premium is durable beyond the contract horizon. Changes in chip architecture through custom base-die migration, CXL tiering, KV-cache disaggregation (Nvidia itself ships disaggregated KV-cache serving), precision compression, none of these break the current LTAs; they just shape what the renewal looks like.

If the differentiated content migrates to customer-specified logic at foundry, the LTAs will have been a well-negotiated bridge to a weaker position for the memory makers: contracted, de-risked, high-volume, they’ll just become merchant DRAM suppliers all over again.

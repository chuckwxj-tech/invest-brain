🇹🇼 Earnings Report – 16 July 2026 – Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC)

Most investors will focus on record quarterly revenue and higher FY26 guidance.

Institutional investors are focused on something much bigger.

TSMC isn't just benefiting from the AI boom—it is expanding capacity because customers are demanding more advanced chips than the company can currently produce.

📊 Q2 2026 Financial Highlights

💰 Revenue: US$40.2 Billion

📈 At the high end of guidance

🎯 Q3 FY26 Revenue Guidance: US$44.6–45.8 Billion

📈 +12% QoQ

📈 +37% YoY

💹 Gross Margin: 67.7%

📈 Operating Margin: 56%+

🏦 Cash & Marketable Securities: US$110 Billion

These numbers reinforce TSMC's unmatched profitability despite unprecedented investment levels.

🚀 AI Demand Is Still Accelerating

Management delivered one of the strongest AI commentaries yet.

Key messages:

✅ AI demand remains "extremely robust."

✅ Agentic AI is creating a new wave of semiconductor demand.

✅ CPUs are becoming increasingly important alongside AI accelerators.

✅ Every major CPU architecture—x86, Arm, and RISC-V—is a TSMC customer.

Management raised its FY26 outlook to:

📈 Revenue growth slightly above 40% YoY (USD basis)—an exceptional growth rate for a company of TSMC's scale.

💰 CapEx Raised Again

This may be the biggest takeaway.

TSMC increased FY26 CapEx to:

💵 US$60–64 Billion

⬆️ from US$52–56 Billion earlier in the year.

Allocation:

• 70–80% → Advanced process technologies

• 10–20% → Advanced packaging, testing & mask making

• 10% → Specialty technologies

Management emphasized that higher CapEx reflects stronger customer demand—not excess capacity.

⚡ Supply Still Can't Keep Up

Perhaps the most important statement on the call:

Management acknowledged that the gap between AI chip demand and available capacity remains very large.

Even with aggressive global expansion, TSMC expects demand to stay exceptionally strong through 2029–2030, driven by AI infrastructure, cloud computing, robotics, and autonomous systems.

🌎 Global Expansion Accelerates

To support long-term demand, TSMC announced:

🇺🇸 Additional US$100 Billion investment in Arizona

Including:

🏭 Multiple new fabs

📦 Advanced packaging facilities

At the same time:

🇹🇼 13 new advanced fabs are under construction in Taiwan.

Management reiterated that expansion plans will continue to be driven by customer demand rather than fixed timelines.

🔬 Technology Leadership Continues

Advanced nodes now dominate production.

Revenue mix:

• 2nm: 3% (Early ramp)

• 3nm: 30%

• 5nm: 33%

• 7nm: 11%

➡️ 77% of wafer revenue now comes from advanced nodes (7nm and below).

Meanwhile, A14 (next-generation process) remains ahead of schedule, with volume production targeted for 2028 and strong customer engagement across smartphone and AI/HPC applications.

📦 Advanced Packaging Remains A Bottleneck

Management admitted that packaging capacity is still constrained.

The shortage is limiting customer growth, prompting continued investment in:

✔️ CoWoS

✔️ Advanced packaging

✔️ Glass substrate technologies

✔️ Larger AI package architectures

Management even welcomed additional industry packaging capacity, saying it would help customers deploy more leading-edge chips faster.

💵 Shareholder Returns Continue To Rise

Despite record investments:

💰 2026 Dividend: TWD 24/share

📈 +33% YoY

Management also indicated another dividend increase is expected in 2027, reflecting confidence in long-term cash generation.

🎯 Institutional Takeaway

This wasn't simply another strong quarter.

It reinforced several structural trends:

✔️ FY26 revenue growth now expected above 40%

✔️ CapEx increased to US$60–64 Billion

✔️ AI demand remains stronger than supply

✔️ Massive global expansion across Taiwan and Arizona

✔️ 2nm production ramping successfully

✔️ Advanced packaging remains a key industry bottleneck

✔️ Confidence to increase dividends while investing at record levels

The most important message from management was clear:

TSMC is not expanding because it hopes demand will come. It is expanding because customer demand for advanced AI chips is already outpacing what the company can manufacture.

If AI infrastructure spending continues over the remainder of the decade, TSMC appears positioned to remain the world's most important semiconductor manufacturer.

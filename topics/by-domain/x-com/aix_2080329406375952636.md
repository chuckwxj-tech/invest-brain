# Executive at Global Electronics Distributor on Marvell Technology $MRVL: "Marvell is rapidly growing in their custom AI scale and accelerator scale, and it's going to be one of ...

## 原文

Executive at Global Electronics Distributor on Marvell Technology $MRVL:

"Marvell is rapidly growing in their custom AI scale and accelerator scale, and it's going to be one of those things that they almost separately disclose because it's such a strong market right now that everybody is looking to that as an indicator. There's so much else that they do, from timing to PCIe to CXL to optical."

> Growth & Sentiment: The channel expert expects sequential quarter-on-quarter acceleration and roughly a 25% to 30% increase in Marvell-related business through calendar year 2026, driven by a strong market super cycle.  

> Primary Revenue Drivers: The majority of revenue is concentrated in data center and AI infrastructure, specifically custom AI accelerators, ASICs, high-speed Ethernet switches (800G and 1.6T), and optical interconnects.  

> Product Transitions:

800G remains a robust high-volume seller, though it faces growing price pressure and new market competitors.  

1.6T products are transitioning rapidly from sampling and qualification into early scalable production ramps.  

3.2T solutions are already being actively sampled by leading customers.  

> Competitive Landscape: Marvell is positioned as a strong number two behind Broadcom. While Broadcom holds massive engineering scale and stringent terms, Marvell wins competitive bids by offering greater flexibility on pricing, lead times, and product supply.  

> Supply Chain Bottlenecks: Standard lead times range from 30 to 42 weeks. The tightest constraints are centered on custom ASICs due to design complexity and severe limitations in advanced substrates and packaging (such as CoWoS).

> Revenue Breakdown: The expert notes that their internal percentage breakdown mirrors Marvell's overall reported revenue. The majority of the business is driven by data center and AI revenue (custom AI accelerators, CPUs, attached silicon at 800G and 1.6T), followed by Ethernet scale-out and switching as the minority.

> Gross Profit & Pricing Adjustments: Marvell's material gross profit hovers around the expected semiconductor range of 45% to 60%. Average selling prices (ASPs) for ASICs and Ethernet switches are up, with year-over-year pricing changes running in the high single-digit to low double-digit range (roughly 9% to 12%).

> Competitive Edge in Power: When customers evaluate Marvell against alternatives, Marvell consistently wins on power per bit (noted as industry-leading) and total cost of ownership (TCO).

> Long-Term Contracts & Order Horizon: Roughly 60% to 30% of the expert's business spans long-term design, technical support, and product longevity cycles versus 30% transactional fulfillment. Driven by supply constraints and risk mitigation, customers are placing orders out 12, 18, 24, and even up to 30 months.

## 原文

If you own $NVDA or $AVGO, the CPO ramp runs through a component most investors have never heard of: the FAU (fiber array unit).

Right now, FAUs for CPO are ~100% single-sourced from one Chinese vendor. The companies working on that single-source problem are Taiwan's iPhone lens makers.

What an FAU does: it connects optical fibers to the optical engine inside a CPO switch. About 5–10% of the BOM, up to $6–7K of content per switch, with a TAM approaching $8B by 2030.

The attached diagram is from Santec, one of the incumbent FAU makers — it shows what's inside: fibers seated in V-grooves, aligned and coupled into the optical engine.

Volume timing, per sell-side supply chain checks: NVIDIA CPO switch shipments ~14K units in 2026 → 190K in 2028 → 1.4M by 2030. The take-off window is 2028–29.

Why this matters for $NVDA and $AVGO holders: second sources are being qualified now. Component makers don't spend two quarters in sampling and certification for a product that isn't coming.

A supply chain preparing for volume is a more direct ramp signal than any bull or bear report. Broadcom's Tomahawk CPO switches need FAUs too.

Who's entering: Largan (3008 TT), the iPhone lens giant, moved from sampling to customer certification after two quarters — faster than other new entrants. Genius Electronic Optical (3406 TT) shipped prism, V-groove and MT ferrule samples to its FAU partner in late May. Initial feedback: no major issues, and three more potential customers are in discussion.

The entry barrier is ±0.3–0.5 micron alignment tolerance. Smartphone optics makers have spent two decades at exactly that scale.

The $GLW angle: the market worries Corning's GlassBridge could replace FAUs. Taiwan sell-side checks suggest it won't in current or next-gen CPO designs — yield challenges above 70 channels, and a GlassBridge solution still needs a fiber array anyway.

For $COHR: Coherent is already on the FAU supplier list, so Taiwan's entry means new competition in a market that barely exists yet.

Watch list for the pure plays: Largan (3008 TT), Genius Electronic Optical (3406 TT), and FOCI (3363 TT) — the FAU integrator the samples flow through.

Micron-level tolerance was built for phone cameras. It's now the entry ticket to AI datacenter optics. $NVDA $AVGO $GLW $COHR

@jukan05 CXL just got its ecosystem reset.

Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron stepping back from in-house CXL controller commercialization does not mean CXL is dead.

It means the Memory Big 3 don’t want to aggressively push a product that could cannibalize their core DIMM cash cow.

The new model looks cleaner:

memory makers focus on DRAM production
fabless firms design the CXL controllers
customers get more flexible/disaggregated architectures
controller value shifts toward Astera Labs, Montage, PrimeMass

This is not retreat.
It is division of labor.

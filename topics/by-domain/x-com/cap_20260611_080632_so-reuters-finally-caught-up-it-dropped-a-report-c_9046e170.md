它发布了一份报告，证实了我几个月来徒劳呐喊的事实：中国对磷化铟衬底的控制是整个 AI 供应链中唯一被低估的地缘政治风险。不是半导体。不是 HBM。甚至不是稀土。而是衬底。

让我带你了解为什么这真的令人恐惧，以及为什么市场仍未将其定价。

The entire future AI datacenter buildout — we're talking 
$NVDA
's next-gen co-packaged optics, 
$GOOGL
's TPU v7 pods, 
$META
 / 
$MSFT
 / 
$AMZN
 hyperscaler clusters — all of it runs on photonics now. Copper is dead at 1.6T. The interconnect backbone of every major AI cluster being built after 2026 requires InP-based lasers and receivers. This isn't speculative. Go look at any TPU or Maia BOM. The optical layer is non-negotiable.
整个未来 AI 数据中心的建设——我们在谈论 $NVDA 的下一代共封装光学、 $GOOGL 的 TPU v7 pods、 $META / $MSFT / $AMZN 的超大规模集群——所有这些现在都依赖光子学。铜在 1.6T 已经过时。2026 年后建设的每一个主要 AI 集群的互连骨干都需要基于 InP 的激光器和接收器。这不是猜测。去看看任何 TPU 或 Maia 的物料清单。光层是不可妥协的。

Now here's the punchline: the global InP substrate market is a duopoly. Two companies control 60–70%+ of supply. 
$AXTI
 — a $700M micro-cap sitting in Fremont, California — owns roughly 30–35%. Sumitomo Electric ($SMTOY, $31.7B) owns another ~30%. JX Nippon chips in 10–15%. That's it. Three suppliers for the material that every optical transceiver, every LiDAR unit, every silicon photonics engine, every hyperscaler interconnect cluster depends on. Yole Group confirmed in 2021 that Sumitomo + AXT together command "more than 75%" of the market. That number hasn't moved.
现在来揭晓重点：全球 InP 衬底市场是双寡头格局。两家公司控制着 60%至 70%以上的供应量。 $AXTI ——这家市值 7 亿美元、总部位于加州弗里蒙特的微型企业——占有约 30%至 35%的份额。住友电工（$SMTOY，市值 317 亿美元）则持有另外~30%。JX 日矿日石占 10%至 15%。就这三家。每一款光收发模块、每一套激光雷达单元、每一颗硅光子引擎、每一个超大规模数据中心互联集群所依赖的材料，都出自它们之手。Yole Group 在 2021 年确认，住友电工与 AXT 合计占据"超过 75%"的市场份额。这个数字至今未变。

What the Reuters report adds is the missing piece I couldn't prove: China controls the raw InP supply chain upstream. AXT's manufacturing is in China. Sumitomo's InP operations are deeply entangled with Chinese rare earth and compound semiconductor supply lines. If Beijing decides to weaponize InP export controls — which Reuters is now explicitly flagging as a live threat — you don't just lose one company. You lose the substrate that 
$AVGO
, 
$LITE

$COHR

路透社的报道补上了我无法证明的缺失环节：中国控制着上游磷化铟（InP）原材料的供应链。AXT 的生产设在中国。住友的 InP 业务与中国稀土及化合物半导体供应线深度纠缠。如果北京决定将 InP 出口管制武器化——路透社现在明确将此列为现实威胁——那么失去的不只是一家公司。你会失去 $AVGO 、 $LITE 、 $COHR 用于 800G/1.6T EML 和 DFB 激光器所需的衬底。你会失去英伟达共封装光学路线图所依赖的连续波激光器阵列。你会失去连接训练集群中每一块 GPU 的光模块。

The downstream blast radius is almost comical: hyperscaler optics, 5G/data transceivers, LiDAR for robotaxis and drones and military, silicon photonics laser dies for Intel and Broadcom's SiPh engines. All of it.
下游的影响范围近乎荒诞：超大规模数据中心光学器件、5G/数据收发器、自动驾驶出租车和无人机及军事领域的激光雷达、英特尔和博通的硅光引擎所需的硅光子激光芯片。全都无法幸免。

And here's the part that keeps me up: the market cap asymmetry is insane. A $700M company is a single point of failure for trillion-dollar AI capex cycles. If export controls hit tomorrow, 
$AXTI
 either becomes the most strategic acquisition target in the semiconductor industry, or it gets nationalized. Either way, the stock rerates violently. The market is still treating this like a niche materials play when it's actually the InP equivalent of ASML — a choke point nobody noticed until the lights started flickering.
而最令我夜不能寐的是：市值不对称性简直离谱。一家市值 7 亿美元的公司，却成为万亿美元 AI 资本开支周期的单点故障。如果明天出口管制落地， $AXTI 要么成为半导体行业最具战略意义的收购目标，要么被国有化。无论哪种结局，股价都会剧烈重估。市场仍将其视为小众材料股，但它实际上是 InP 领域的 ASML——一个直到灯光闪烁才被察觉的咽喉要道。

HBM got all the attention in 2024–2025. InP substrates will be the bottleneck story of 2026–2027. Reuters just handed everyone the map.
HBM 在 2024–2025 年备受瞩目。InP 基板将成为 2026–2027 年的瓶颈故事。路透社刚刚为大家指明了方向。

I've been holding this thesis since the original deep dive. The report doesn't change the math — it just means the clock is now ticking louder.

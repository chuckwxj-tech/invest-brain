What's happening - July 22nd AM

Astera Labs expands its Taurus retimer family to 3.2T speeds. $ALAB  (Astera Labs) announced the industry's first OCP-standard footprint compatible 3.2T smart retimers and redrivers, extending its Taurus platform. The product is purpose-built for scale-up networking in AI racks, directly competing with $AVGO (Broadcom), $CRDO  (Credo Technology), and $MRVL (Marvell). As GPU cluster sizes grow, the need for high-density, low-latency connectivity at the rack level is accelerating. Astera's expansion positions it to capture more of the interconnect content inside AI server pods.

$AMD  unveiled a reference architecture built around its MI400 series and 6th-gen EPYC CPUs. AMD (Advanced Micro Devices) partnered with Cornelis Networks on a design for AI inference, training, and HPC workloads. The reference architecture uses AMD Instinct MI400 series GPUs and EPYC CPUs, providing hyperscalers and OEMs a validated path to deploy AMD-based clusters. This is a direct challenge to NVIDIA's DGX/HGX dominance and signals growing ecosystem readiness. For the supply chain, broader AMD adoption means increased demand for socketed CPU/GPU packages (benefiting AMAT (Applied Materials) and $KLAC (KLA Corp)), higher-density printed circuit boards (impacting $TTMI (TTM Technologies) and $SANM (Sanmina)), and more liquid cooling requirements as MI400 power envelopes scale.

Arteris IP is being deployed by Speedata for data center compute SoCs. AIP (Arteris) announced that its network-on-chip interconnect technology is used in Speedata's data center inference chips. Arteris is a confirmed supplier to AMD, and its IP is increasingly found in custom AI accelerators and smart NICs. The Speedata win underscores the trend toward specialized silicon for AI inference, which in turn drives demand for Arteris' FlexGen and FlexNoC IP. For the broader buildout, more custom silicon designs mean more tape-outs, benefiting CDNS (Cadence) and SNPS (Synopsys), and more test and burn-in demand for companies like AEHR (Aehr Test Systems).

CoreWeave Cloud is powering real-time photorealistic AI avatars for Anam. CRWV (CoreWeave) announced that its GPU cloud platform is being used by Anam to render photorealistic avatars in real time. This validates CoreWeave's positioning as a compute provider for inference-heavy, latency-sensitive workloads. As CoreWeave scales its fleet, it drives procurement of NVIDIA GPUs, networking gear, and data center infrastructure. Companies such as VRT (Vertiv) and J (Jacobs) could benefit from CoreWeave's continued buildout of its data center portfolio.

ADTRAN reported preliminary Q2 results, citing strength in optical transport. ADTN (ADTRAN Holdings) released preliminary second-quarter 2026 results, though exact figures were not provided in the release. ADTRAN competes with CIEN (Ciena) and NOK (Nokia) in metro optical and data center interconnect. Any positive revenue inflection from ADTRAN would signal growing demand for DCI gear as AI traffic drives bandwidth requirements between data center sites.

## 原文

Liqid, a provider of software-defined memory and GPU pooling infrastructure, has appointed Daniel Berg as Chief Product Officer. Berg brings more than 30 years of product and engineering leadership, including executive roles at Gorilla Logic, JumpCloud, @NetApp , @Skype , and Sun Microsystems.

At @WeAreLiqid, he will lead product management, strategy, and engineering across the company’s portfolio, including Liqid Matrix and its software-defined memory and GPU pooling technologies.
His appointment comes as Liqid expands its OEM relationships and focuses on the infrastructure constraints facing neocloud providers and enterprises deploying AI workloads.

Berg most recently served as CEO of Gorilla Logic, a nearshore software services company. Before that, he was CTO of JumpCloud, a cloud-based identity and unified IT management platform.

Earlier, he served as Vice President and General Manager of NetApp’s SolidFire business unit following NetApp’s acquisition of SolidFire, where he had been Chief Product Officer. Berg also held the roles of Chief Technology Officer and head of Research and Development at Skype.

He spent 15 years at Sun Microsystems in engineering and management positions and was named a Distinguished Engineer. Berg has written several books and papers on Java, concurrent programming, software development methodology, and software architecture. He has also advocated for greater diversity in engineering and for continuous integration and deployment practices that improve collaboration and software delivery.

Liqid’s technology pools and dynamically allocates CXL memory and GPUs across data center environments, allowing infrastructure resources to be assigned and scaled according to workload demand. The company is targeting inefficiencies associated with conventional server architectures, where memory is tied to individual systems and GPUs may remain underutilized between jobs.

Those limitations are becoming more visible as AI inference, KV cache workloads, and in-memory databases place greater pressure on server memory capacity and accelerator utilization.
Liqid CEO Rick Hegberg said Berg’s background is particularly relevant as the company moves its technology toward broader production use.
“Dan has built products at companies where technical execution and operational reliability were critical,” Hegberg said. “He understands how to turn complex technology into products that infrastructure teams can deploy and manage. That experience will be important as memory and GPU pooling become part of more mainstream infrastructure buying decisions.”
Berg said the central challenge for modern AI infrastructure is not only cost, but also rigidity and low utilization.

“Modern AI infrastructure is expensive, inflexible, and often poorly utilized,” Berg said. “Liqid addresses that problem at the fabric level, working alongside hardware that neocloud providers and enterprises already own.”
He added that his experience building product organizations around complex infrastructure problems would shape his work at Liqid.

“I have spent much of my career building product organizations around difficult infrastructure challenges, and I look forward to applying that experience at Liqid,” Berg said.

Berg’s experience across storage, cloud identity, and real-time communications infrastructure will help shape Liqid’s product roadmap as the company works with OEM and ecosystem partners including @AMD , @Cisco, @DellTech , Micron, @nvidia , and Penguin Solutions. The broader objective is to make memory and GPU pooling easier to deploy within neocloud and enterprise infrastructure.

## 原文

I’ve been taking a close look at $LPK.DE LPKF’s latest H1 2026 report.

It confirms a lot of what I was feeling when I decided to exit my position.

Don't get me wrong: as a long-term technology thesis, LIDE and glass substrates are the real deal. 

The structural shift toward glass for Advanced Packaging and AI chiplets is genuinely exciting. 

LPKF raising its TAM expectation to €1.7B by 2030 and expanding into multi-layer glass bonding and Co-Packaged Optics (CPO) shows their tech is top-notch.

However, as investors, we need to separate TAM from reality:

TAM vs. SOM & Financial Pressure:

> Market Size Reality Check: 

A €1.7B TAM looks great on presentation slides, but LPKF’s actual Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) will be a fraction of that once competitors, yields, and customer dual-sourcing kick in. 

> CEO Reality Check: 

Management explicitly noted that while negotiations for ramp-up phases are ongoing, it is "premature to conclude that a firm breakthrough in the supply chain has been achieved."

> H1 Numbers Under Pressure: 

Revenue dropped 38.3% YoY to €36.5M (mainly due to Solar collapsing from €22.2M to €4.4M as clients wait for perovskite tech), with Adjusted EBIT at -€10.4M. 

> Execution Costs: Restructuring costs under their "North Star" efficiency program are eating up 3–4% of annual revenue as headcount gets trimmed.

The market got way ahead of itself on the glass substrate narrative, pricing in the TAM without accounting for the smaller SOM and the long timeline.

High-volume commercial scaling is still a 2027+ story, and in the meantime, the company is navigating a messy transition phase.

Honestly? 

I'm really glad I locked in my gains on $LPKF when I did - bagging nearly a 180% return on that run. 

Leaving the party early felt hard at the time, but watching the market come back down to reality makes me appreciate the exit even more.

I’ll keep $LPKF on my watchlist because the LIDE tech is too strategically important to ignore forever. 

But for now? 

I’m more than happy sitting on the sidelines and watching how they execute on their 2028 margin targets before even thinking about getting back in.
(Especially considering that I sold this position at the end of April and used the proceeds to buy $PENG, which went on another +100% rally for me.)

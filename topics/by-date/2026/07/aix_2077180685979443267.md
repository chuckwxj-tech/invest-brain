BofA: Optical Devices (800G & 1.6T Outlook)

Demand: 800G expected 50–60M units in 2026; 1.6T 30M units. Industry to satisfy 70–80% of 800G and 50–60% of 1.6T demand in 2026. 800G flattens in 2027, while 1.6T grows at least +50%.

SiPh Penetration: 50% for 800G and 60% for 1.6T by 2026.

Form Factors: Pluggable transceivers remain mainstream through 2030. NPO/XPO volume starts 2027; 3.2T pluggables in 2028.

Copper vs Optics: Scale-up (NVIDIA GPU-to-GPU) relies on short-reach copper through 2028. Optics dominate scale-out. AECs critical for 5–7m reaches at 1.6T.

Constraints: Tight supply in EML, DSP, and faraday rotators; laser shortages into 2027. Tier-1 suppliers with scale/prepayments may ease in 2H26.

Hyperscaler Profiles:

AWS: Low-cost focus, ramping 800G.

Google: Leads in optical switching (OCS), tripling/quadrupling volume in 2026–27.

Meta: Ramping 800G (>10M units), open to CPO/LPO.

Microsoft: Distributed model (70–80km links), slow-and-wide macro LED.

China Cloud: 2–3 years behind, heavy on 400G due to regulations/DSP limits.

Supplier Landscape: Tier-1 leaders (Innolight, Coherent, Eoptolink) expected to maintain >50% share. ($MSFT $GOOGL $AMZN $NVDA $TSEM $BABA $STM $CRDO $SMTC $APH $AAOI)

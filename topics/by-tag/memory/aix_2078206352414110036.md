Alright, let's start with $VIAV

Market cap: ~$9.2B
Next-quarter guide: ~$432M (+6.2% QoQ, +48.7% YoY)
Next-12M revenue: $1.80-1.95B, 32-43% growth
Exit margins: 63-64.5% gross, 24-26% operating

VIAVI sits in the test layer for the optical buildout

They touch many different stages of testing

> During lab and product development
> During manufacturing and production
> During installation, operating, and field management

They have very broad exposure in the data center:

> Traditional pluggables
> LPO, LRO, NPO, CPO, XPO 
> AECs 
> Retimers
> PCIe 
> CXL 
> Hollow Core Fiber

So they can test the silicon-photonics component, the optical engine, the packaged module, the Ethernet system, the physical fiber network, and the live AI fabric.

One thing that I really like is that complexity increases test intensity. CPO, NPO, and advanced packaging create more testing insertions per system, so they can grow from both higher network volumes and more test content attached to each build.

Also their Network and Service Enablement (NSE) segment has gross margins above 65%, with management estimating that roughly 40–45 cents of each incremental revenue dollar can reach operating profit.

Some risks: Spirent integration, weak cash conversion/dilution, multiple compression, and delays in 1.6T/CPO customer ramps.

*Reported growth includes the acquired Spirent product lines, which contributed $54.2 million last quarter, so don’t treat all of that growth as organic.
*Margins / Rev are my projections

4 AMD closed up 8.11%, with a turnover of US$14.86 billion. On July 20, local time, AMD publicly unveiled its first rack-level AI system Helios to the global media for the first time, marking its official launch of a full-stack data center AI infrastructure solution and a comprehensive impact on the AI computing market dominated by NVIDIA.

This integrated AI platform that deeply integrates software and hardware has won orders from many global technology giants and is expected to begin large-scale deployment in 2026.

AMD predicts that this business will bring tens of billions of dollars in data center AI revenue to the company starting in 2027, rewriting the competitive landscape of the global AI computing power market.

According to AMD's official disclosure, Helios, named after the ancient Greek sun god, is a set of standardized and open rack-level AI reference design solutions. It is not a single server product, but a full-link AI infrastructure system covering computing power, scheduling, network, and software.

The system is deeply integrated with AMD's four self-developed core modules, including the new generation of Instinct series GPUs, EPYC series CPUs, Pensando high-speed network chips and ROCm full-stack software platform, realizing deep adaptation and collaborative optimization of software and hardware, breaking the pain point of traditional AI computing equipment software and hardware separation.

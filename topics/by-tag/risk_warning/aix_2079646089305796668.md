One that doesn't get much coverage, $FN

Market cap: ~$19.1B
Next-quarter guide: ~$1.27B (+4.6% QoQ, +39.6% YoY)
My next 4q revenue estimate: $5.5–6.0B, 30–40% growth
My exit margins: 12.5–13% adjusted gross, 11–12% adjusted operating

Fabrinet sits in the manufacturing layer.

Rather than betting on one laser, DSP, module supplier or architecture, FN manufactures across the ecosystem.

Their exposure includes:
> Optical components and subassemblies
> 800G and 1.6T modules
> DCI and coherent optics
> CPO and external light sources (ELS)
> Optical circuit switches (OCS)
> Advanced packaging

The current momentum is already strong as they grew revenue 39% last quarter to $1.21B, while DCI revenue grew 90%. Datacom demand also exceeded shipments because lasers, memory and advanced chips remained supply constrained. 

That means supply improvement alone can unlock additional revenue.

Two direct hyperscaler 800G programs have completed qualification and begun shipping, while merchant transceiver programs should add another layer of growth through fiscal 2027.

Outside optics, HPC board revenue is also ramping and should eventually exceed $150M per quarter.

As customers outsource more packaging, assembly, testing and full-system integration, FN captures more revenue from each program without needing to own the underlying product road map.

The capacity build shows how management is thinking about the opportunity. Building 10 and other expansions should raise annual revenue capacity toward roughly $8.5–9B, leaving significant room above the current run rate.

The margin profile looks thin at first glance, but that is the contract-manufacturing model.

Adjusted gross margin was 12.1% last quarter, while operating expenses remain extremely low. That allows FN to convert a 12–13% gross margin into roughly an 11% operating margin.

The balance sheet is also strong, with about $945M of cash and short-term investments and no financial debt at the end of the quarter. 

The biggest risks are customer concentration, component shortages, working-capital intensity, capacity execution and customers’ products failing to ramp as expected.

Revenue and margins are my projections.

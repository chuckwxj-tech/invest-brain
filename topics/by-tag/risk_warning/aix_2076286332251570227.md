# NVIDIA's 88-core Vera CPU with native CXL 3.1 over PCIe Gen 6 and 1.8 TB/s NVLink-C2C was designed to bridge proprietary NVLink to open rack-scale pooling, offloading Agentic AI...

## 用户备注

## 原文

NVIDIA's 88-core Vera CPU with native CXL 3.1 over PCIe Gen 6 and 1.8 TB/s NVLink-C2C was designed to bridge proprietary NVLink to open rack-scale pooling, offloading Agentic AI KV caches from costly HBM to cheaper DDR5 to prevent Rubin GPU bottlenecks and enable dynamic memory composition via fabric switches and controllers from Astera Labs and Marvell, targeting $200 billion server CPU market. 

That bet is now stalled by ecosystem delay: Samsung has pushed its 1TB, 72GB/s CXL 3.1 module mass production to Q1 2027 plus, with SK Hynix and Micron also pausing CXL 3.1 to stay on CXL 2.0, because CXL 3.1 requires PCIe 6.0 hosts that are absent as Intel Diamond Rapids slips to Q2/Q3 2027 and AMD Venice sampling arrives only post-September 2026. Vera will therefore launch early into a vacuum, forcing NVIDIA to rely on CMX and CXL 2.0 interim while hyperscaler-wide pooling slips to 2027 to 2028.

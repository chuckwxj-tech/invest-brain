🚨 AI's Next Bottleneck Isn't GPUs—It's Memory, Says Morgan Stanley

— 🧠 Morgan Stanley believes the next major challenge for AI is the "Memory Wall," where powerful GPUs spend more time waiting for data than processing it.

— 📈 Memory is projected to grow from 12% of cloud infrastructure spending today to 40% by 2030, making it one of the biggest investment themes in AI.

— 💾 SK Hynix leads the AI memory race with over 50% of the High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM) market, positioning it as a key supplier for next-generation AI systems.

— 🚀 Micron Technology is rapidly expanding its AI footprint through long-term HBM supply agreements and AI-optimized DRAM, benefiting from sustained hyperscaler demand.

— 🔗 Marvell Technology offers a different AI play through CXL memory expansion, enabling AI servers to share memory more efficiently as workloads become increasingly complex.

— 💡 The next wave of AI investment may shift from buying more GPUs to building faster, larger, and smarter memory infrastructure, creating new winners beyond Nvidia.

— 👀 As hyperscalers continue pouring billions into AI, memory suppliers and infrastructure companies could become some of the biggest beneficiaries of the next AI growth cycle.

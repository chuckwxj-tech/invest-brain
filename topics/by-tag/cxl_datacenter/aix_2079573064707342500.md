SAMSUNG AND SK HYNIX BET ON CXL 3.2: CHEAPER MEMORY EXPANSION FOR THE AI ERA

TLDR: CXL will not replace HBM, but Samsung and SK Hynix see it as a cheaper way to expand AI-server memory and move less-used data out of expensive HBM.

CXL connects additional DRAM through PCIe, allowing servers to expand memory beyond fixed motherboard slots. It is slower than local DRAM, but frequently used data can stay in faster memory while colder data moves into lower-cost CXL modules.

Samsung plans to upgrade its CMM-D 3.0 product to CXL 3.2 and begin mass production by year-end. SK Hynix has already shown a second-generation CMM-DDR5 sample, while both companies are working toward greater control over the controller chips inside these modules.

The timing still depends on compatible CPUs, including AMD’s EPYC Venice and Intel’s Diamond Rapids. CXL remains a small early-stage market, but it could help memory makers reduce AI infrastructure costs and use HBM more efficiently.

Now one of my favorite companies, $COHR

Market cap: ~$57.2B
Next-quarter guide: ~$1.98B (+9.6% QoQ, +29.5% YoY)
My next-4Qrevenue estimate: $8.5–9.0B, 29–36% growth
My exit margins: 40.5–42% adjusted gross, 23–25% adjusted operating

Coherent has some of the best overall balance in the optical group.

Their exposure includes:
> 800G and 1.6T transceivers
> EML and CW lasers
> Optical circuit switches
> CPO and external light sources
> ZR/ZR+ and DCI
> Pump lasers and amplifiers
> Telecom systems

Datacenter and Communications now represents roughly 75% of revenue and grew 41% year over year on a pro forma basis.

800G remains strong, while 1.6T should ramp hard through late 2026 and 2027.

Optical circuit switches (OCS) are another major driver.
They worked through a production bottleneck and is now expanding OCS output across two facilities. 

Initial CPO revenue should also begin contributing, while ZR/ZR+, pump lasers, amplifiers and transport systems give them broad exposure to DCI and scale-across.

One thing I really like is the vertical integration.

COHR makes the lasers, detectors, silicon photonics, optical components, engines, complete modules, switches and transport systems.

That gives them more ways to capture content from each connection and more control over their supply chain.

The six-inch indium-phosphide transition is also a major part of the margin thesis. Management says six-inch wafers can produce more than 4x as many devices at less than half the cost per device versus three-inch wafers.

If yields remain strong and the factories fill, that should help move adjusted gross margin above 40%.

NVIDIA invested $2B directly into COHR and entered a multiyear agreement that includes multibillion-dollar purchase commitments and capacity rights through 2030.

The biggest thing I am watching is cash conversion.

Capex and working capital remain elevated as COHR expands capacity. The margin improvement now needs to begin translating into free cash flow.

At roughly 6.4–6.7x my next-four-quarter revenue estimate, I think COHR offers one of the better combinations of growth, product breadth, improving margins and valuation in the group.

Risks: six-inch yield execution, capex and working capital, OCS/CPO timing, industrial weakness and multiple compression.

Revenue and margins are my projections.

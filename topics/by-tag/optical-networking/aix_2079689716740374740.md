JPMorgan on Coherent ($COHR): CEO meetings point to a broader AI optical stack story

JPMorgan says Coherent’s investor meetings reinforced that this is not just a datacom transceiver cycle. The bigger point is COHR trying to own more of the AI optical stack — TRx, CPO/NPO content, optical circuit switching, pump lasers, and eventually higher-ASP subsystems.

Key proof points:
• Datacom TRx demand still exceeds supply, and 1.6T is ramping before 800G has even hit peak volumes
• CPO revenue timing remains scale-out in 2H26 and scale-up in 2H27 — possibly being pulled forward
• COHR expects InP device output to double by 3Q26, then more than double again in CY27, effectively 4x in two years
• The 6-inch InP ramp is already yielding ahead of mature tech, with substrate supply locked across five suppliers
• OCS SAM was recently doubled to $4B, but JPM thinks that could still be conservative as use cases expand beyond scale-out into DCI/scale-across and scale-up

Why it matters:
The bear worry is that CPO eventually cannibalizes pluggable transceivers. JPM/management frame it differently: TRx stays relevant for standards-based scale-out, while CPO/NPO creates another content lane where COHR can sell far more than just lasers.

CPO / NPO angle:
COHR highlighted content beyond CW lasers and ELS modules: VCSELs, isolators, thermoelectric coolers, PICs, photodetectors, FAUs, PM fiber, and micro lens arrays. In aggregate, those pieces can equal or exceed the ELS dollar content, and COHR is often one of few qualified suppliers.

Capacity / margin angle:
The InP expansion should translate to transceiver output with a 2–3 month lag. Management also reiterated a “greater than 42%” gross margin target, with upside from price optimization, 6-inch cost benefits, and higher-margin products like CPO, OCS, multi-rail, and thermadite.

Other callouts:
• Pump lasers remain scarce; COHR is one of two high-quality suppliers and has ~70% share in some types
• Multi-rail revenue is on track for 1H27, with COHR pursuing components, modules/line cards, and full systems
• Selling line cards or systems could mean 10x+ ASP uplift vs components
• Thermadite ramps in 2H27 and is 2–5x more efficient than copper heat sinks/cold plates, potentially lifting XPU utilization by 20–30%+
• Semi-cap industrial demand is improving, with lasers/materials seeing ~6-month lead times

JPM view:
Overweight on COHR. The meeting takeaway is pretty clear: JPM sees Coherent as a scarce-content AI optics supplier with multiple shots on goal, not a one-product transceiver trade.

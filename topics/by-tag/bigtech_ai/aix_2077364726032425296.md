⠀
Almost every engineer kept bringing up the same keyword — CXL.
⠀
If you’re not paying attention to CXL yet, you could be missing the most important shift coming to the storage industry.
⠀
📄Meta presented a paper titled “Vistara: Making CXL Real” at ISCA 2026, the top conference in computer architecture.
⠀
The paper reveals that Meta’s servers typically have a lifespan of only 3–5 years, while memory components can last 7–10 years.
⠀
At the same time, roughly 40% of servers can no longer be expanded with additional memory, pushing Meta to start reusing retired DDR4 modules.
⠀
The technology making this possible is CXL.
⠀
📌This piece breaks down several key questions:
⠀
What DRAM, HBM, PCIe, and CXL each actually do, and how they divide the work.
⠀
Why GPU compute keeps getting faster while data movement is increasingly the real bottleneck for AI systems.
⠀
Why CXL isn’t a replacement for HBM, but could still help break through the long-standing “memory wall.”
⠀
Where companies like Marvell fit into this emerging supply chain.
⠀
💡After talking with multiple engineers, a consensus is forming: storage and high-speed interconnect are converging faster, and the two tracks have reached a key inflection point.
⠀
🏗️Storage vendors are also changing in nature — no longer just suppliers of DRAM or NAND chips, but increasingly embedded in the system-level design of AI training and inference clusters, becoming a core part of AI infrastructure itself.
⠀
🎯My core view: CXL may not be the fastest technical path, but it’s likely to become an infrastructure route that future AI systems can’t avoid.
⠀
The storage sector is breaking free of its old cyclical-stock valuation framework, and the reason goes well beyond tight supply or a pricing upcycle.
⠀
Storage vendors like $MU are seeing their value increasingly embedded in the co-design of AI chips and system architecture.
⠀
So I’d argue the market’s focus going forward shouldn’t just be DRAM shipment volumes, or debating whether the price upcycle runs through 2028 or 2030.

What really matters is that as storage, interconnect, and compute converge, a whole new era of AI infrastructure is taking shape — one that will keep generating new incremental opportunities.

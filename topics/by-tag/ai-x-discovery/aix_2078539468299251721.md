Astera Labs’ AI infrastructure role helped Alger in Q2

What this means
Here's the thing: Alger Capital Appreciation is still riding the “AI plumbing” trade, and Astera Labs was a positive contributor. ALAB designs high-speed connectivity silicon for AI and cloud data centers — PCIe, CXL, Ethernet gear that moves data cleanly between GPUs, CPUs, and memory at rack scale. Q1 results beat on revenue as hyperscalers bulked up AI capacity and pulled the latest connectivity products. Stock closed $350.62 on July 15 (~$60B market cap): -16% over one month, +258% over 52 weeks. Basically: heart of the buildout, still a roller coaster.

Hidden points
- Bottleneck story isn’t only GPU supply — it’s signal integrity and data movement inside massive server fabrics
- Expanding hyperscaler relationships + growing product portfolio = the Alger bull case
- Fund beat Russell 1000 Growth in Q2 on IT and communication services
- Hedge-fund holders: 53 portfolios at end of Q1 vs 52 prior — still not a top consensus “most popular” name in one tracker

Real impact / takeaways
Every GPU cluster needs high-speed interconnects; Astera sells into that necessity. Demand tracks hyperscaler capex with a lag, so AI spend pauses show up here too. Product cycles and design wins matter as much as the AI slogan. After a multi-bagger year, monthly 16% drawdowns are the price of admission.

Investor / trader take
High-beta AI connectivity pure play — own for secular rack-scale buildout, size for semi-style vol. Prefer adds on broader AI-infra selloffs if hyperscaler guides stay firm. Watch design-win breadth beyond one customer and next earnings for sequential growth. Peer-check against other connectivity names (Credo, etc.) on multiples and concentration risk.

Bottom line
Astera Labs helped Alger’s Q2 as hyperscale AI capacity drove a revenue beat — connectivity chips at the center of the data-center buildout, with a stock that still swings hard.

## 原文

There are several ways to stack advanced chips.

MR-MUF, TC-NCF/TCB, micro-bumps, silicon interposers, and fan-out packaging are already used in mass production.

So why is Samsung reportedly preparing to introduce a hybrid-bonding line at Pyeongtaek?

Not because hybrid bonding is the only solution.

It is because the industry is approaching the point where conventional bumps begin to run out of margin.

As HBM moves toward taller stacks—and logic, memory, and photonic chips are integrated more tightly—package height, interconnect pitch, power consumption, and thermal resistance become the new constraints.

Hybrid bonding removes the solder bumps and directly joins copper to copper. That enables thinner stacks, denser I/O, and lower-energy die-to-die connections.

The exact adoption timeline may still move. Conventional bonding could remain viable for another generation.

But the direction is clear:

The next semiconductor bottleneck is shifting from transistor scaling alone to assembly precision and bonding capacity.

# Goldman Sachs projects that SiPh penetration in the datacom market will increase nearly 8x, from 6% in 1Q24 to 46% in 4Q28E, driven by the adoption of new hardware architectures...

## 原文

Goldman Sachs projects that SiPh penetration in the datacom market will increase nearly 8x, from 6% in 1Q24 to 46% in 4Q28E, driven by the adoption of new hardware architectures like Blackwell and Vera Rubin. ER from $AEHR demonstrates the velocity of this transition, with their FY2027 Revenue Outlook of $130 million to $150 million significantly outperforming market expectations.

Further highlighting this momentum, $AAOI  rose 12% today after announcing construction has begun on two adjacent properties in Pearland. This expansion will add approximately 400,000 square feet of manufacturing capacity to boost the output of 800G and 1.6T optical transceivers used by industry leaders like Nvidia, $META , and Google. Given Meta's aggressive expansion plans, they are highly likely to be the primary driver behind this capacity increase.

Specifically, as the Goldman Sachs "Attach Rate" analysis (Exhibit 38) shows, Meta’s custom ASIC architecture (MITA-T V1 / Minerva) utilizes a highly complex Disaggregated Scheduled Fabric . Due to this unique networking topology, Meta requires a massive attach rate of 1:8 to 1:12 optical modules per chip. To put this in perspective, Meta’s total demand for 800G/1.6T optics is estimated at 10 to 12 million units, the largest overall volume requirement among North American hyperscalers.

In contrast, while Google is also a major volume buyer of high-speed optics, it remains highly reliant on InnoLight, which is projected to capture up to 80% of Google's 1.6T/800G orders to support its OCS Apollo networks. 

Similarly, Microsoft maintains deep partnerships with Coherent and InnoLight. This competitive dynamic leaves AAOI's primary tier-1 cloud relationships structurally aligned with Meta and Microsoft, making Meta the most logical anchor customer for AAOI’s massive Pearland expansion.

Everyone's trading hybrid bonding. Not many can explain it.

In the ever-evolving world of semi conductors, hybrid bonding is the "new" thing this year. 

What is the fuss about? Is it really transformational? How can you play this theme? Bookmark this for the answers.

> Problem: Modern AI chips are stacked. To talk to each other, the layers connect through microbumps: tiny balls of solder. Today's best microbump pitch is ~40µm (a 25µm bump with a 15µm gap). Solder takes space, adds resistance, adds heat, and you can only shrink it so far.

> Hybrid bonding kills the solder bump. Instead of stacking chips on tiny balls of solder, you polish two copper surfaces flat and fuse them directly, pad to pad. Denser interconnects, shorter distances, less heat. It's how you go truly 3D.

> Why it matters: 
- Pitch: ~40µm → under 10µm (~4x finer). Shorter gap = the signal travels less distance, so it's faster and loses less energy on the way.
- Density: up to ~15x more interconnects per mm². More wires between layers = more parallel data lanes in the same area. Bandwidth without buying more silicon.
- Energy: ~3x more efficient per bit. In a datacenter you don't just buy the chip once, you also have to pay to move data across it every second. Power IS the cost.
- Bandwidth: 3D stacks clearing 1,000+ GB/s. That's the number that feeds a GPU fast enough to keep it from starving.

> Importance of this: Every micron removed between two dies does three things at once: cuts the distance electrons travel, cuts the energy to send each bit, and frees area for more connections. Distance, power, density: one process step improves all three.

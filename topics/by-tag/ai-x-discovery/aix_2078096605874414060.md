NEWS: India Plans Full-Scale Semiconductor Ecosystem with Semicon 2.0 - a $15B Buildout 

The Cabinet just approved the next big chapter of the India Semiconductor Mission. Building on Semicon 1.0, this is a holistic, long-term plan to build the entire ecosystem from design to manufacturing.

Six key pillars:
- Deepening chip design 
- Machines, materials & chemicals 
- Attracting more fabs (silicon, compound, display etc., first one by 2028)
- Boosting advanced packaging (ATMP/OSAT)
- R&D for advanced nodes
- Massive talent development

Semicon 1.0 already greenlit projects worth Rs 1.64 lakh crore. $MU Micron, Kaynes & CG Semi have started commercial production, with more coming in 2026.

$INTC Intel is already deeply embedded, contributing to global 18A chips, exploring advanced packaging (Odisha glass substrate project with 3DGS), and in talks for DLI 2.0 design incentives. 

Semicon 2.0 strengthens the local talent pool, supply chain, and incentives, which is a perfect tailwind for Intel’s foundry ambitions, AI hardware, and potential packaging / manufacturing collaborations in India.

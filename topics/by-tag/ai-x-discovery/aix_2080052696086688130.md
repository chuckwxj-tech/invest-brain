JPMorgan on Coherent ($COHR): investor meetings with CEO Jim Anderson

JPMorgan says Coherent’s AI optical story is broader than “does CPO kill pluggables?” The cleaner read is that datacom transceivers, CPO/NPO components, optical circuit switching, pump lasers, and thermal materials are all tightening at once — and COHR has content across most of the stack.

Key proof points:
• Datacom transceiver pricing remains healthy, with demand still above supply
• 1.6T is ramping before 800G has even peaked, helping mix/margins
• COHR still sees CPO revenue ramping in scale-out in 2H26 and scale-up in 2H27
• InP device output is expected to double by calendar 3Q26, then more than double again in 2027
• Substrate supply is locked across five suppliers, including multiple geographies and 6-inch capability

Why it matters:
The bear question is whether CPO eventually cannibalizes optical transceivers. JPMorgan’s meetings suggest COHR is less exposed to that binary than people think: pluggable TRx remains preferred for standards-based scale-out, while CPO/NPO still pulls in a lot of COHR content.

CPO / NPO angle:
COHR highlighted content beyond CW lasers and external laser sources: VCSELs, isolators, thermoelectric coolers, PICs, photodetectors, FAUs, PM fiber, and prism micro lens arrays. Management said those components can be similar to or greater than the ELS dollar content, and NPO content is largely consistent with CPO — the main difference is where the optical engine attaches.

Capacity angle:
The 6-inch InP transition is tracking ahead of the mature process, with transceiver output lagging device output by ~2–3 months. That matters because the 1.6T/AI datacom ramp is still supply constrained, not demand constrained.

Other upside pockets:
• OCS SAM was recently doubled to $4B and JPMorgan thinks it may still be conservative
• Scale-up could become the biggest long-term OCS use case
• COHR is one of two high-quality pump laser suppliers, with ~70% share in certain types
• Pump laser scarcity creates chances to move up-stack into line cards or systems at 10x+ ASPs
• Thermadite ramps in 2H27 and is described as 2–5x more efficient than copper heat sinks/cold plates, potentially lifting XPU utilization 20–30%+

Margin angle:
COHR continues to target >42% gross margin, with JPMorgan noting hints of a higher reset as the target period approaches. The drivers are price optimization, 6-inch cost improvement, and richer-margin products like CPO, OCS, multi-rail, and thermadite.

JPMorgan view:
Overweight. The meeting reinforces COHR as one of the cleaner “AI optics bottleneck” plays: not just a transceiver vendor, but a component/platform supplier across the optical and thermal stack.

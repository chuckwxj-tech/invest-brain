@DudeWhoInvests The pivot is smart. Here's why the data backs it:

What GOOGL just told us (from their Q1 2026 10-Q):

"To  meet the AI compute capacity demands of our customers, we are engaging  in the supply of our custom hardware which may increase our costs and  operational complexity. We also have a number of large, long-duration  commercial agreements… backstops to support the build-out of third-party data centers and power infrastructure."

Translation:  Google can't build fast enough. They're underwriting OTHER people's  data centers. That's a direct pipeline to infrastructure names.

The capex numbers tell the story:

Cerebras?  Revenue growing fast but forecasting negative full-year margins and  just dropped 10% on their earnings debut (per CNBC, 24 Jun 2026). High  conviction, high execution risk.

The hyperscalers remain the best  risk/reward. But when GOOGL literally files that they're backstopping  third-party data center buildouts, the overflow trade has legs. Just  size it appropriately — these are dabble positions, not core holdings.

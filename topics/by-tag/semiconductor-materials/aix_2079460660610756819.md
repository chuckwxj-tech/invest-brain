## 原文

TSMC unfazed by Japanese supplier halt.

Taiwan’s semiconductor giant says it sees “no impact” on operations after Japanese specialty gas maker Central Glass announced plans to stop producing high-purity tungsten hexafluoride (WF6). 

The gas is essential for etching and depositing tungsten in advanced chip manufacturing.

The reason? A shortage of raw materials from mainland China. While the news raised brief supply-chain concerns, TSMC moved quickly to reassure stakeholders.

In an official statement, TSMC highlighted its robust global procurement strategy: “We continuously develop diversified supply solutions and maintain a broad supplier base worldwide.” Multiple alternative sources for tungsten-related specialty gases are already available, and current inventory levels remain stable.

This episode underscores both the fragility and resilience of today’s semiconductor ecosystem. Geopolitical tensions and concentrated raw-material sources can create sudden ripples, yet leading foundries like TSMC have spent years building redundancy exactly for moments like these.

In an AI-driven world hungry for chips, supply-chain agility is not optional, it is survival.

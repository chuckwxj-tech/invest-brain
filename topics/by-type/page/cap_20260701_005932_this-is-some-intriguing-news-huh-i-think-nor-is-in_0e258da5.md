## 原文

This is some intriguing news, huh?  
I think NOR is in pretty short supply too, though.
这是个有趣的新闻，对吧？  
不过我觉得 NOR 闪存的供应也挺紧张的。  

Following warnings from major Chinese memory chip manufacturers, Taiwanese companies such as Macronix, Winbond, and Nanya Technology are also heightening their vigilance.
继中国主要内存芯片制造商发出警告后，台湾地区的旺宏、华邦和南亚科技等公司也纷纷提高警惕。  

GigaDevice is a leading company driving the rapid growth of China's memory industry. Not only does it boast an outstanding track record in the NOR flash market, but it has also expanded its business into niche memory technologies such as SLC NAND, DDR3, DDR4, and LPDDR4X. It is listed on both the Shanghai Stock Exchange and the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, holding a significant position in the industry. Additionally, it manufactures microcontrollers (MCUs), sensors, and automotive electronics.
兆易创新是中国存储行业快速增长的领军企业。不仅在 NOR 闪存市场有着出色的业绩，还将其业务扩展到 SLC NAND、DDR3、DDR4 和 LPDDR4X 等利基型存储技术领域。该公司同时在上海证券交易所和香港证券交易所上市，在行业中占据重要地位。此外，它还生产微控制器、传感器和汽车电子产品。

GigaDevice's memory products overlap heavily with those of Macronix and Winbond, and they directly compete with some product lines from Nanya Technology.  

Before this memory market entered a "supercycle," GigaDevice had been leveraging price competition to secure orders, putting pressure on Taiwanese manufacturers like Winbond and Macronix.  

This time, GigaDevice has issued a warning that "prices could drop significantly," drawing major attention from the market.  

According to GigaDevice's announcement, the memory chip industry has always exhibited clear cyclicality. Depending on macroeconomic conditions, industry cycles, and shifts in market supply and demand, prices for major products could decline sharply in the future. In such a scenario, not only would sales prices for the company's products fall, but gross margins would also be severely impacted, affecting overall profitability and posing a risk of deteriorating company performance.  

Amid the market's overwhelmingly bullish sentiment toward the future of the memory industry and rising expectations of continued price increases, GigaDevice has thrown cold water on the sector, shocking the industry.  

Industry analysts believe that the recent surge in memory prices is primarily driven by strong demand for AI servers. Major international memory makers have shifted resources toward high-value-added products like high-bandwidth memory (HBM), high-end DRAM, and high-layer-count 3D NAND chips, leading to constraints on supply for mature processes and niche memories. This has resulted in ongoing shortages of NOR flash, SLC NAND, and niche DRAM.  

However, GigaDevice views the niche memory market as distinct from mainstream DRAM and NAND markets. Downstream demand is relatively stable, with primary applications spanning not just mobile phones, PCs, and servers, but also consumer appliances, industrial controls, networking equipment, and automotive electronics.  

This price surge stems more from indirect benefits of strategic shifts by major memory makers than from a sudden explosion in end-user demand.  

Influenced by the warning message regarding potential product price declines, GigaDevice's stock fell yesterday (the 30th) on both mainland China and Hong Kong exchanges, dropping 2.9% and 4.2%, respectively.
受产品价格可能下滑的警示影响，昨日（30 日）兆易创新在沪深及港股市场均出现下跌，跌幅分别为 2.9%和 4.2%。

In Taiwan's manufacturing sector, Macronix and Nanya Technology bucked the broader market's sharp 1,126-point rally, closing lower. Macronix suffered a net sell-off of over 40,000 shares by foreign investors, while Nanya Technology's stock also declined by more than 18,000 shares. Winbond Technology dipped about 3% at one point but closed up 4.5 Taiwan dollars at 207.5 Taiwan dollars. However, it faced a net sell-off of around 22,000 shares by foreign investors.
台湾制造业中，旺宏电子和南亚科技逆势下跌，未跟随大盘暴涨 1126 点的走势，最终收跌。旺宏电子遭外资净卖出逾 4 万张，南亚科技股价也下跌超过 1.8 万张。华邦电子盘中一度下跌约 3%，但收盘上涨 4.5 元新台币，收于 207.5 元新台币，不过也遭外资净卖出约 2.2 万张。

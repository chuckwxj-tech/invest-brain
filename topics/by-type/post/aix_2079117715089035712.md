## 原文

The memory Big Three stepping back from proprietary CXL controllers is not bearish for CXL.

It may be one of the clearest bullish signals yet for the fabless layer.

According to a Korean industry report, Samsung, $SKHY and Micron have scaled back or scrapped plans to commercialize their own CXL memory-expansion controllers. Micron and SK Hynix reportedly withdrew more directly, while Samsung removed commercialization from its roadmap but retained limited internal research.

The reason matters.

Memory companies originally wanted to bundle proprietary controllers with DRAM and sell an expensive, high-margin finished product—essentially trying to recreate the HBM model.

Hyperscalers wanted the opposite:

→ A modular controller architecture
→ Compatibility with standard DIMMs
→ Freedom to mix memory suppliers
→ Lower costs and less vendor lock-in
Once the controller and DRAM are separated, memory makers lose much of the differentiation. Worse, an expensive integrated CXL product could cannibalize the conventional DIMM business that already generates enormous revenue.
So this is not CXL demand disappearing.
It is CXL evolving from a vertically integrated memory product into an open, fabless-led ecosystem.

The cleanest direct beneficiaries may be Montage Technology and $ALAB, whose controllers can be paired with memory from multiple suppliers. Montage’s CXL 3.1 controller is already sampling, with Samsung and SK Hynix publicly supporting deeper collaboration. Micron is also validating its DRAM with Astera Labs’ CXL controller platform.

$MRVL is slightly different.

It is not simply replacing one proprietary controller. Marvell is building a broader CXL fabric spanning memory-expansion controllers, switches, retimers and rack-level memory pooling. That gives it exposure to the entire architecture rather than only one component.

The market structure is becoming clearer:

Memory companies manufacture the DRAM.
Fabless companies increasingly control the logic connecting it.

The real confirmation will not come from another roadmap announcement.

It will come from hyperscaler qualifications, controller attach rates and volume shipments.

CXL is not being abandoned.
The profit pool is simply moving.

## 原文

🇨🇳 Flagging this unusually surgical semi-conductor related move of China:

China restricted the high-purity tungsten powder Japan needs to make WF6, while Chinese exports of finished WF6 can still continue. Japanese producers are being starved of feedstock while Chinese producers can still sell the higher value finished material. China shipped no tungsten powder to Japan from February through April, yet continued shipping WF6 during the first five months of 2026.

The forced sequence this creates:

1) Japanese WF6 supply becomes unreliable and Samsung, SK Hynix and other fabs cannot wait 12 to 18 months.
2) They accelerate qualification of Chinese WF6, helping Chinese suppliers clear a process that normally takes years of testing, trust and customer relationships.
3) Once qualified inside semiconductor production, those suppliers become harder to replace even if China later restores normal tungsten powder exports.

China may be using the threat of shutdown to force Chinese materials deeper into foreign semiconductor fabs and push chipmakers to qualify Chinese suppliers.

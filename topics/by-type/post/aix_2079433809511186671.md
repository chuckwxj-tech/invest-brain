Street Signal | Apple's AI server chips face challenges, which may benefit Nvidia (Morgan Stanley)

The Morgan Stanley report pointed out that Apple's AI server chips (including M2 Ultra and the first-generation ASIC "Baltra") may face performance challenges, which may cause Siri AI to perform less than expected, or force Apple to use more expensive NVIDIA (NVDA) chips in Google Cloud.

The first-generation Baltra ASIC is planned to be mass-produced in small quantities in the first half of 2027, and the second-generation ASIC is expected to be launched in 2028. The market has high expectations for Apple's AI capabilities, but chip-level challenges may delay the large-scale deployment of its AI capabilities.

If Apple switches to Nvidia chips, it will directly increase Nvidia's GPU demand and may undermine market confidence in Apple's ability to develop its own chips.

One-sentence conclusion: The potential performance issues of Apple's AI server chips may hinder the advancement of its AI strategy in the short term and may bring additional data center orders to Nvidia. However, in the long term, the evolution of Apple's self-developed chips will still be a key variable. Positive/negative: Positive for Nvidia (NVDA).

If Apple increases purchases, there will be new demand. It is negative for Apple (AAPL). If its AI capabilities lag behind expectations, it may affect its service revenue and the appeal of high-end hardware. Catalysts:

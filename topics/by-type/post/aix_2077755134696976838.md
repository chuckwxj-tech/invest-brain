UBS: Materials Sector

Following a stock price correction, market focus is shifting toward products with strong price-hike visibility. UBS ranks the visibility of price hikes in the second half of 2026 from highest to lowest as follows:

MLCC Materials > MLCCs > WF6 > Semiconductor Wet Chemicals > Helium

Multi-Layer Ceramic Capacitors (MLCCs)

> Tight Supply-Demand: Tight supply-demand for high-capacitance MLCCs is expected to persist in H2 2026, driven by robust demand from AI servers crowding out capacity.  

> Price Increases: Major manufacturer Yageo raised prices effective July 1, 2026, across automotive, industrial, AI server, and consumer electronics sectors.  

> Upstream Materials: Upstream material suppliers are operating at high utilization rates, offering further upside potential for material prices (e.g., release films).

Electronic Specialty Gases

> Tungsten Hexafluoride (WF6): Market prices for 5N-grade WF6 doubled year-to-date (YTD) to Rmb1,800/kg. This surge is primarily due to a sharp decline in China's exports of tungsten powder (the key raw material), leaving Japanese and South Korean manufacturers facing shortages.  

> Helium: After peaking in April due to Russian export controls and issues in Qatar, helium prices corrected down to Rmb250/cubic meter. Prices are expected to stabilize between Rmb200–300/cubic meter in H2 2026.

Electronic Wet Chemicals & Targets

> Hydrofluoric Acid (EG-HF): Rising raw material costs (such as sulfur and anhydrous hydrogen fluoride) combined with strong export demand are expected to continue driving price hikes.

Co-packaged optics/CPO is not actually a photonics problem...it is really a packaging problem.

The hard part is putting optical engines next to v hot AI chips - without destroying yield or reliability.

That is why the Intel angle is interesting.

TSMC’s CoWoS uses giant interposers. Powerful, but expensive, thermally difficult, reticle-constrained, and harder to scale as packages get larger.

Intel’s EMIB uses small local silicon bridges instead. You localize the high-density connection only where needed, which could make optical I/O easier to manufacture and cool.

If CPO becomes a packaging war, im thinking that Intel’s silicon photonics history plus EMIB may matter more than people think.

共封装光学/CPO 实际上并非光子学问题……它其实是一个封装问题。

难点在于将光学引擎置于非常热的 AI 芯片旁——同时不损害良率或可靠性。

这便是为何英特尔的角度值得关注。

台积电的 CoWoS 采用巨大的中介层。功能强大，但成本高昂、热管理困难、受光罩限制，且随着封装尺寸增大而更难扩展。

英特尔的 EMIB 使用小型局部硅桥接代替。你只在需要的地方进行高密度连接本地化，这可能使得光学 I/O 更容易制造和冷却。

如果 CPO 成为一场封装战争，我认为英特尔的硅光子学历史加上 EMIB 可能比人们想象的更重要。
